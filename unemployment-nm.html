Unemployment Insurance Modernization — New Mexico

Role: Project Manager | Company: Deloitte | Period: Oct 2012 – Mar 2013

Delivered New Mexico’s first integrated tax and benefits system. Led documentation, operations manuals, and readiness activities to support statewide rollout and achieve client acceptance for a major modernization initiative.

Overview

As Project Manager, I supported the delivery of New Mexico’s first fully integrated Unemployment Insurance tax and benefits system. This effort replaced legacy processes with a unified platform designed to improve accuracy, compliance, and operational efficiency across the state’s UI program.

My responsibilities focused on preparing the program for production readiness — including documentation, operational manuals, training materials, and acceptance processes — ensuring that the state had the tools and clarity needed to operate the new system effectively.

Key Outcomes

  • Supported delivery of New Mexico’s first integrated UI tax and benefits system
  • Produced comprehensive operational manuals and documentation for statewide use
  • Facilitated client acceptance through structured readiness reviews and walkthroughs
  • Improved cross‑team alignment between Deloitte, state leadership, and technical teams

Responsibilities

  • Developed and delivered operational manuals, process documentation, and user‑facing materials
  • Coordinated readiness activities, including walkthroughs, reviews, and acceptance checkpoints
  • Worked with functional and technical teams to validate system behavior against requirements
  • Supported defect triage, issue resolution, and stabilization efforts
  • Provided status updates and risk assessments to program leadership

Project Scope

  • Integrated tax and benefits processing
  • Employer registration and contribution reporting
  • Benefits eligibility, adjudication, and payment workflows
  • Compliance and federal reporting
  • Operational readiness and statewide rollout support

Tools & Methods

  • Operational documentation and process modeling
  • Readiness assessments and acceptance frameworks
  • Cross‑functional coordination and stakeholder engagement
  • Structured testing and defect management processes

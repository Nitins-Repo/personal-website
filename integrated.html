Lead M&O for the Ohio Benefits Program, responsible for availability, performance, deployments, and vendor coordination across Worker, Self-Service, Provider, Presumptive Eligibility portals, EDMS, CSS/IVR, automation, and BI services.

Overview

Serve as the primary M&O lead for the Ohio Benefits Program with responsibility for system availability, performance, deployments, and vendor coordination across Worker, Self-Service, Provider, Presumptive Eligibility portals, EDMS, CSS/IVR, automation, and BI services.

Key outcomes

  • Sustained 99.9% system availability across program components
  • Managed 300+ batch programs and integrations with 50+ partners
  • Oversaw IVR/contact center handling of ~4M annual calls; help desk ~3,000 monthly
  • Supported 12K+ internal users and 3M active beneficiaries

Responsibilities

  • Operational monitoring, incident management, and root-cause analysis
  • Release and deployment coordination, patching, and performance tuning
  • Vendor and stakeholder engagement, and disaster recovery planning

EDMS (Enterprise Document Management)

Led the accelerated deployment of an Enterprise Document Management Solution across multiple HHS programs within a six-month timeframe, achieving high user adoption and streamlining document workflows for case processing and retention.

IVR & Contact Center

Spearheaded the Shared Services rollout and IVR implementation that enabled clients to access benefits via a single number and submit applications using audio signatures; oversaw IVR operations and help desk processes.

Automation & Batch Operations

Managed automation (BOTS) and 300+ batch programs supporting nightly processing and integrations with 50+ partners to ensure timely eligibility and benefits processing.

BI & Reporting

Maintained BI portal capabilities for operational reporting, analytics, and performance monitoring to support decision-making and SLA adherence.

Portals

Oversaw Worker, Self-Service, Provider, and Presumptive Eligibility portals—responsible for uptime, accessibility, and feature deployments.
